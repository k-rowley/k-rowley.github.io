Alice in Wonderland

by Lewis Carroll

✦

It all began on an afternoon that should have been quite unremarkable. Alice sat in the sunshine, dreaming her small, daydreamy thoughts — until a white rabbit ran by in a waistcoat, checking a pocket watch as though he were late for something very important. She followed, of course. Wouldn’t you?

One moment, she was leaning into a rabbit hole; the next, she was falling — slowly, curiously — past cupboards and maps and jars of marmalade. Below lay a place where nothing stayed the same size for long, where tiny doors hid whole worlds, and where every label was a dare. These first three chapters are the opening notes of a much stranger song, and the only way to hear it is to...
